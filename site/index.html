Notifier Service

As of the 2019-20 school year, Notifier is no longer generally available to the public. This is due to the potential security concerns associated with Notifier -- most school districts are not keen on having a third party storing educational records, much less user credential data. While we only use credential data to occasionally check grades (in the same way a service like EarlyScores only uses your credentials to check AP scores on your behalf), there isn't really a way to "prove" we're not doing anything else. Therefore, the safest way to prevent issues is to not offer such functionality to the general public in the first place.

OpenVUE by default does not store any personally identifiable information (including your grades) other than the bare mininum outlined in the privacy policy. However, this is not possible if you want OpenVUE to notify you on new tests / assignments. Thus, using Notifier requires a greater scope on the amount of data collected (and thus, additional consent).

Due to this, out of an abundance of caution, any new users to OpenVUE by default will not be able to opt-in to the notifier service.

If you joined OpenVUE during its open beta period, you are automatically whitelisted. Otherwise, if you would like access and do not currently have access, contact svuelegal [at] itsryan.org or me in person. A form with written and signed consent may be required.

Loading GPA/Course History

Why will it take a while to load?
If you've used the official StudentVUE mobile app, you may have noticed that course history is missing from it. That's because the endpoint that the mobile app uses doesn't support course history, and is the same endpoint OpenVUE normally uses.

So how do we work around this limitation? When you select "Load Course History", the OpenVUE server will open up the StudentVUE web interface, sign in as you, and extract the course history data before finally signing you out and discarding your session token. While this works (and works reliably), it's a three-step process (fetch login page, login, fetch course history page) which means it'll take a while to complete. It also means that when you select "Load Course History", you'll see a new entry under "Login History" on the official website - that's the server.

Fortunately, this process only has to be done once on each device you use, because your device will save it locally after it gets the data. (It's not ever stored server-side to avoid legal trouble - course history is considered a sensitive educational record!)

Notification Categories

Note that each category also includes anything placed into a higher category. For example, selecting I≥1 will also include tests, projects, and final exams.

I≥0: Everything (Not Recommended)
You'll get a notification every time something is put into gradebook. Not recommended, as notifications are rate-limited to one every few hours due to bandwith limitations, and everything will get grouped together.

I≥1: Important Items (Default)
You'll get a notification every time an "important" item is put in. These include quizzes, labs, challenge problems, etc. Anything that's highlighted in dark blue is considered an "important" item.

I≥2: Tests and Projects
You'll get a notification only when tests and projects are put in. These items are highlighted in a brighter shade of blue in Gradebook.

I≥3: Final Exam Only (Not Recommended)
You'll only get a notification when your final exam for a class is put in. Not recommended, just turn off notifier if you don't want notifications.

Grade Rounding

Synergy rounds grades in each class to 0-2 decimals (this is determined by the teacher).
Your "raw" grade is a more accurate grade in the class, and is used in the grade calculator to give you results with a higher precision than third party grade calculators.

Attendance Module / Icon Legends

The attendance icons used in OpenVUE are designed to be similar to the ones used in standard StudentVUE for better consistency. Note that you can also hover over (on desktop) an icon to see what it means.

Activity: School excused activity. Differs from standard "excused" in that this mark only applies to school-sponsored activities.
Not Included: Attendance is not tracked/included for this course.
Excused: School excused. Generally has no negative impact.
Absent: In most cases, this mark is functionally equivalent to "Excused".
Excused Tardy: Tardy, but excused. Also generally has no negative impact.
Unexcused Tardy: You're tardy for a non-academic reason. Some schools may give disciplinary actions for excessive unexcused tardies.
Unexcused: You didn't show up. StudentVUE sends automated calls and/or emails when this happens.

Tip: You can expand individual dates to see details by clicking / tapping on such section.
